Biography

Charlotte Jolly is a clarinet and saxophone player and vocalist based in Suffolk. She is musically diverse and in demand as a chamber musician, singer, song-writer and avid improviser. Since graduating from Trinity Laban in 2009 with a first class degree in the clarinet, she has had a busy performance career performing with Lontano Ensemble, Perhaps Contraption, Hetty and the Jazzato Band, Tafahum Ensemble, Wilderness Orchestra, Brighton Philharmonic, the New Music Players, and as a session musician on world-wide tours, radio performances and festivals with groups including These New Puritans, Ebony Bones and the Sparks at venues ranging from Fuji Rock Festival to Glyndebourne.

Charlotte is a key member (tenor saxophone and vocals) of the award-winning brass collective Perhaps Contraption recording 3 albums, performing internationally and more recently working co-creatively on a 2 year Open Space residency at Britten Pears Arts. She is also a founding member of Hetty and the Jazzato Band, video amassing more than 25 million views for their arrangement of Tu Vuo Fa L’Americano and many performances in the UK and beyond.

Charlotte has played in many improvisation groups including Folie a deux Femmes with harpist Fontane Liang. Their co-created work for narrator, clarinet and harp ‘Two Parts Mischief’ was performed at Just So Festival and has been released with an illustrated children’s book. Their improvisation in response to Stravinsky’s Rite of Spring has been featured on BBC Radio 4.

Charlotte is passionate about performing as an onstage musician in theatrical work including 2 projects with Spitalfields Musical Rumpus (operas for 0-2.5 year olds) and performing as part of an onstage band at Shakespeare’s Globe with Deafinitely Theatre in their performance of A Midsummer Nights’ Dream. She also continues to work as a songwriter and workshop leader for educational theatre projects.

New projects include working as a lead vocalist with Mikibos with Mickey Bones from Perhaps Contraption, clarinet noodler and vocalist with The Three Nightingales, a local group performing pre 1960’s music from America and her brand new solo project Beekabop, an Early Years show for 0-5 year olds first performed at the Aldeburgh Festival in 2019. She also works as an educator and mentor for Britten Pears Arts and their Aldeburgh Young Musicians programme.
